Protecting Equity in the Schools of Tomorrow Process

Equity is at Risk

The San Jose Unified School District's "Schools of Tomorrow" process threatens to undermine educational equity and disproportionately impact our most vulnerable communities.

Our Concerns

  • Lack of Transparent Process: Decisions affecting our children's futures are being made without adequate community input and transparency.
  • Disproportionate Impact: Proposed changes will disproportionately affect students from underserved communities and families of color.
  • Resource Inequity: The process fails to address systemic funding disparities and instead compounds existing inequalities.
  • Community Voice Ignored: Parents, teachers, and students are being sidelined in favor of top-down decision making.
  • Rushed Timeline: Critical decisions are being rushed without proper analysis of long-term equity impacts.

What's at Stake

The "Schools of Tomorrow" initiative could fundamentally reshape our district in ways that:

  • Reduce access to quality education for historically marginalized students
  • Increase barriers to educational opportunity
  • Eliminate programs that serve our most vulnerable populations
  • Widen achievement gaps rather than closing them
